Filipino Chicken Asado

Description:
Filipino Chicken Asado features tender chicken chunks or pieces, typically drumsticks or thighs, cooked in a rich and sweet tomato-based sauce. The sauce is a harmonious blend of flavors, combining the sweetness of sugar and the tanginess of vinegar, with hints of soy sauce for a savory umami kick. This dish is often garnished with bell peppers and sometimes with boiled eggs, adding color and additional flavor to the dish. The result is a delightful balance of sweet and savory that Filipinos love.

Recipe for Filipino Chicken Asado:

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of chicken pieces (drumsticks or thighs)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced (optional)
  • 2 boiled eggs (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Asado Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (white or cane vinegar)
  • 1/2 cup tomato ketchup
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Saut√© the garlic and onions until the onions are translucent.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides for about 5 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, ketchup, water, brown sugar, bay leaf, and black pepper. Mix well to create the asado sauce.
  4. Pour the asado sauce over the browned chicken in the pot.
  5. Cover the pot and let it simmer over medium heat for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and fully cooked. Stir occasionally.
  6. If you're using red bell pepper, add it to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking to add flavor and color.
  7. If you're using boiled eggs, you can add them whole or cut in half to the pot during the last 5 minutes to soak up the sauce.
  8. Taste the sauce and adjust the sweetness or saltiness according to your preference. You can add more sugar, salt, or vinegar as needed.
  9. Once the chicken is tender, and the sauce has thickened to your liking, remove the bay leaf, and your Filipino Chicken Asado is ready to serve.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Enjoy your homemade Filipino Chicken Asado, a delightful mix of sweet and savory flavors that's sure to be a hit at your dinner table!
