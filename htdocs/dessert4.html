Filipino Biko

Description:
Filipino Biko is a traditional Filipino rice cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and brown sugar. It's a sweet and sticky dessert with a caramelized topping. Biko is often served on special occasions and is a favorite treat during celebrations and gatherings.

Recipe for Filipino Biko:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup coconut cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Wash the glutinous rice and cook it with coconut milk and salt in a rice cooker or a heavy-bottomed pot until fully cooked.
  2. In a separate pan, melt the brown sugar over low heat until it caramelizes and turns into a syrup. Be careful not to burn it.
  3. Spread the caramelized sugar on the bottom of a greased pan or banana leaves (traditional method).
  4. Add the cooked glutinous rice on top of the caramelized sugar and press it down firmly.
  5. Drizzle coconut cream on top of the rice and spread it evenly.
  6. Steam the biko for about 30-40 minutes until it sets and becomes sticky.
  7. Let it cool, then slice and serve.

Enjoy the sweet and sticky goodness of Filipino Biko, a delightful dessert that's perfect for special occasions!
