Pancit

Pancit is a popular Filipino noodle dish, and one of the most well-known types is Pancit Canton, a stir-fried noodle dish. Here's a description and step-by-step guide for cooking Pancit Canton:

Ingredients:

- 8 ounces of Pancit Canton noodles (or any egg noodles)

- 2 tablespoons cooking oil

- 1/2 pound of chicken breast, sliced into thin strips

- 1/2 pound of pork belly, sliced into thin strips

- 1 small onion, chopped

- 3 cloves of garlic, minced

- 1 small carrot, julienned

- 1/2 small cabbage, shredded

- 1/2 cup green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces

- 1/2 cup red bell pepper, sliced into thin strips

- 1/4 cup soy sauce

- 1/4 cup oyster sauce

- 1 cup chicken or pork broth

- Salt and pepper to taste

- Calamansi or lemon wedges for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the noodles: Boil the Pancit Canton noodles according to the package instructions, usually for about 2-3 minutes. Drain and set aside.

2. Stir-fry the chicken and pork: Heat the cooking oil in a large wok or pan over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and pork strips and stir-fry until they are lightly browned and cooked through. Remove them from the pan and set them aside.

3. Sauté the aromatics: In the same pan, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until they become fragrant and translucent.

4. Add the vegetables: Add the julienned carrot, green beans, and sliced red bell pepper. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

5. Combine the noodles and sauce: Return the cooked Pancit Canton noodles to the pan. Pour in the soy sauce and oyster sauce. Toss the noodles and vegetables to coat them evenly with the sauce.

6. Pour in the broth: Add the chicken or pork broth to the pan. This will help to cook the noodles and create a flavorful sauce.

7. Add the cabbage and meat: Add the shredded cabbage and the cooked chicken and pork back into the pan. Stir everything together.

8. Season and simmer: Season the dish with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the Pancit Canton to simmer for a few more minutes until the cabbage wilts and the sauce thickens.

9. Serve: Transfer the Pancit Canton to a serving platter. Garnish with calamansi or lemon wedges, if desired.

Pancit is often served as a festive dish and is a staple at celebrations and gatherings in Filipino culture. It's a flavorful and satisfying noodle dish that can be customized with various ingredients and flavors to suit your taste. Enjoy your homemade Pancit Canton!
